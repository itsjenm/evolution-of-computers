Evolution of Computer Languages

Year Computer Languages Author Description State of use
1952 Autocode In 1952 Autocode was developed for the Mark 1 computer at the University of Manchester. It was the first language that could be translated into machine code.
1958 FORTRAN Joh Backus John Backus created FORmula TRANslation or FORTRAN in 1957. Used today in supercomputers and scientific and mathematical computations.
1958 ALGOL and LISP American and European scientists ALGOL stands for ALGOrithmic Language. It became the point of origin for world-renowned programming languages such as Pascal, Java, C, and C++. In the same year, John McCarthy invented the List processor or LISP. Intended for AI, companies like Boeing and Genworks are still using it.
1959 COBOL Developed by a team led by Dr. Grace Murray Hppper COBOL stands for Common Business Oriented Language. It was designed for credit card transaction processors, traffic signals, and phone calls. Used today in banking and gaming. Today's virtual PBX system and other modern solutions to communication
1964 BASIC Developed by Dartmouth College students It stands for Beginners All-Purpose Symbolic Instruction Code. BASIC was then further developed and marketed as product by Bill Gates.
